Solar Energy

What Can Solar Panels Do?

Solar photovoltaic (PV) systems, often known as solar electric systems, collect and convert sunlight energy into electricity. PV systems may power everything from lights and appliances to an electric vehicle in your home.

History of Solar Energy

The first solar panels, invented in the 1800s, were less than one percent efficient, not nearly enough to make them a reliable energy source. It wasn’t until 1954 that Bell Labs invented the first useful silicon solar panel, which was about six percent efficient. Since then, solar PV technology has evolved at a rapid pace. Manufacturers have been able to create prototype solar panels that are over 30 percent efficient!

How to find the efficiency of Solar Electric Systems

To calculate the efficiency of a solar panel, you must use:

Efficiency (%) = (Pmax ÷ Area) ÷ (1000) x 100%

where, Pmax = solar panel peak power (in Watts) Area = length x width of the solar panel (in sq.m.) 1000 = Standard Test Condition (STC) irradiance (in Watts/sq.m.)

Maintenance

Hire a qualified solar contractor to conduct a detailed preventative maintenance inspection annually or bi-annually. A detailed inspection will include:

  • A visual and mechanical inspection of the solar array
  • An electrical inspection
  • Interior inspection
  • Battery maintenance
  • Infrared inspection of electrical connections
