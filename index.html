How does a wind turbine produce electricity?

The aerodynamic force of the blades, like an airplane, converts wind energy into electricity. The air pressure on one side of the blade lowers when wind blows across it. Lift and drag are created by the difference in air pressure across the two sides of the blade. The lift force is greater than the drag force, causing the rotor to spin. The blades are attached to a drive shaft that rotates an electric generator, which provides power.

How do we harness power from the sun?

In an hour and a half, the amount of sunlight that penetrates the earth's surface is enough to power the entire world's energy usage for a year. Photovoltaic (PV) panels are mirrors that concentrate solar radiation. They are used in solar technologies to convert sunlight into electrical energy. This energy will be converted into electricity, and stored in a battery for long term use.

How is energy produced with water>

Hydroelectric power stations are frequently built on or near water sources such as waterfalls, rivers, and artificial bodies of flowing water. The amount of accessible energy in moving water is determined by the volume of the water flow and the elevation change, as water is commonly known to head from one location to another. Head is the vertical height measured between the hydro intake water level and the water level at the point of discharge. In general, the higher the head, the greater the water flow, this increases the amount of electricity a hydropower plant can generate.

Cool Facts about Renewable Energy!

  • Wind

    Turbines are becoming taller in order to reach higher in the atmosphere, where quicker, more consistent winds can be found. The higher the turbine, the faster the winds will be, and the more energy it will be able to generate!

  • Hydro

    Hydropower costs less than most energy sources. States that get the majority of their electricity from hydropower, like Idaho, Washington, and Oregon, have energy bills that are lower than the rest of the country.

  • Solar

    World Health Benefits from solar power According to Google's Project Sunroof, which combines data from Google Maps and Google Earth with 3D modeling and machine learning, 79 percent of all homes in America have rooftops that are "technically viable" for solar, meaning they have enough roof area and receive enough sunlight.
