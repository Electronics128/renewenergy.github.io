Wind Energy

What Can Wind Turbines Do?

Small wind electric systems are one of the most cost-effective home-based renewable energy systems — with zero emissions and pollution — if you have adequate wind resource in your area and the scenario is suitable. These systems can provide lifetime savings!

How Wind Energy has evolved

The Wind Energy Technologies Office (WETO) collaborates with industries to enhance the performance and resilience of next-generation wind technologies while minimizing wind power costs. The office's research works have benefited in increasing the average throughput (a measurement of electricity generation production) for wind turbines manufactured prior to 1998 from 22% to over 35% currently, up from 30% during the 2000's. Wind energy costs in the United States have plummeted from over 55 cents per kilowatt-hour (kWh) in 1980 to an average of under 3 cents per kWh currently. Wind industry technologies must keep progressing in order to secure future industry size, building on previous triumphs to improve reliability, enhance capacity factors, and reduce costs.

How to Plan for Small Electric Wind Systems

You'll need to evaluate your site's average annual wind speed, or wind resource, to assist determine its feasibility for a small electric wind system. Because of local geography, local structures, and vegetation affects on wind speed and flow, the wind resource can vary dramatically over a 1 square mile area. To determine whether your location has enough wind resource to warrant calling an installer for a resource estimate, review a wind resource map, which might show whether you reside in a suitable wind resource region.You should examine local zoning rules and community covenants before investing in a small wind energy installation. Contact the local building inspector, board of supervisors, and/or planning board to learn about zoning regulations in your region. They can inform you if you'll need a building permit and provide you a list of everything you'll need. Your neighbors or homeowners' association may protest to a wind turbine that limits their view, in addition to zoning difficulties. They could also be bothered by noise. The majority of zoning and aesthetic issues can be resolved by providing objective data.

Maintenance

You should hire a professional installation to install your system. Additional services, such as permitting, may be provided by a reputable installer. Check to see if the installer is a licensed electrician, and ask for and check references. You should also contact the Better Business Bureau. A small wind electric system can last up to 20 years or more with proper installation and maintenance. For Example you may consider these simple maintenance techniques to enhance your small wind system.

  • Bolts and electrical connections should be checked and tightened as needed.
  • Checking for rust on machinery and ensuring that the guy wires are properly tensioned
  • If necessary, inspect and replace any worn leading edge tape on the turbine blades.
  • As needed, replace components such as turbine blades and/or bearings.
