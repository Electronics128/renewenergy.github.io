How to Plan for Small Electric Wind Systems

To plan for a Wind System, you must consider the following

  • You'll need to evaluate your site's average annual wind speed
  • Examine your local geography, local structures, and vegetation (these factors will affect wind speed and flow
  • You might want to call a installer for a estimate and to know whether your location is eligible
  • Review Local rules and regulations
  • Contact a local building inspector and or a board of supervisors

Maintenance

You should hire a professional contractor to install your system. Additional services, such as permitting, may be provided by a reputable installer. Check to see if the installer is a licensed electrician, and check for reliable credentials. You should also contact the Better Business Bureau. A wind-electric system can last 20 years or more with proper installation and maintenance. For example, you may consider these simple maintenance techniques to enhance your small wind system.

  • Bolts and electrical connections should be checked and tightened as needed.
  • Checking for rust on machinery and ensuring that the guy wires are properly tensioned
  • If necessary, inspect and replace any worn leading edge tape on the turbine blades.
  • As needed, replace components such as turbine blades and/or bearings.
