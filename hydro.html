What Can Hydroelectric Power Do?

Hydropower converts kinetic energy into electricity using turbines and generators, which is then supplied into the electrical grid to power homes, companies, and industries.

How to Plan for a Microhydropower System

You'll need flowing water on your land to develop a micro-hydropower system. A substantial amount of falling water is required, which usually, but not always, indicates that hilly or mountainous locations are the best. A potential micro-hydropower site's power output, economics, permits, and water rights are all factors to consider. To discover if a micro-hydropower system is right for you, you'll need to figure out how much power you can get from the running water on your property. Maintenance

Despite the fact that a hydropower plant is designed to endure decades, harsh weather and typical wear and tear do take their toll on its many components:

  • Turbines suffer abrasions from suspended solids.
  • Metals are more prone to corrosion in the watery environment.
  • High pressure, stress cycles, and water hammers often cause fatigue cracking.
  • Unbalanced and misaligned shafts cause vibration and wear out bearings.
