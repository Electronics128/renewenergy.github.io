Hydro Power

What Can Hydroelectric Power Do?

Hydropower converts kinetic energy into electricity using turbines and generators. They are then supplied to the electrical grid to power homes, companies, and industries.

How to Plan for a Microhydropower System

You'll need flowing water on your land to develop a micro-hydropower system. A substantial amount of falling water is required, but not always. Hilly or mountainous locations are the best. A potential micro-hydropower site's power output, economics, permits, and water rights are all factors to consider. Maintenance

Although a hydropower plant is designed to endure decades of harsh weather, typical wear and tear do take their toll on many main components:

  • Turbines suffer abrasions from suspended solids.
  • Metals are more prone to corrosion in the watery environment.
  • High pressure, stress cycles, and water hammers often cause fatigue cracking.
  • Unbalanced and misaligned shafts cause vibration and wear out bearings.
